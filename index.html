Eighteenth Century Recipes

Custard Sauce

Custard sauce is a common ingredient for british desserts in the eighteenth centuury, as well as today. The steamed puddings of the time were savory, as sugar was cost prohibitive. The custard sauce provides the necessary sweetness without overpowering the puddings. The addition of rose water gives the sauce a nice eighteenth century touch, and differs from the standard sauces you may see in the UK today.

Ingredients

Ingredients Amount
egg yolks 4
sugar 1/4 cup
salt pinch
milk 1 3/4 cup
heavy cream 1/4 cup
rose water 4 drops
brandy 1 1/2 tbsp

Method

  1. In the top of a double boiler, beat the eggs yolks with the sugar and the salt.
  2. Add the milk and the cream and stir well.
  3. Set the pot over (not in) gently boiling water and cook, stirring constantly, until the custard is thick enough to coate the back of a spoon (about 10 minutes).
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the rose water and brandy

Note: This sauce can be served hot or cold. It will thicken as it cools.

Spotted Dog

Spotted dog is the classic example of steamed pudding of the nineteenth century. Easy to make, a tad savory with nice fruity notes from the currants, and a beautiful presentation make this a must have dish for anyone practicing historical cooking. The dish is also referred to as spotted dick.

Ingredients

Ingredients Amount
flour 4 cups
sugar 1/4 cup
salt 1/2 tsp
ground cinnamon 1 1/2 tsps
dried currants 1 3/4 cups
ground nutmeg 1/4 tsp
suet (finely grated) 1/2 lb.
milk 1 cup
eggs (lightly beaten) 2

Method

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg
  2. Stir in the currants, breaking them apart (the flour will coat them and keep them from clumping together)
  3. Mix in the suet
  4. Add the milk and eggs and work the mixture thoroughly with your hands
  5. Scrape the batter into a greased 6-cup pudding-basin
  6. Tie a well-floured cloth over the pudding. Place the pudding in a pot of boiling water, cover, and steam for 2 hours
  7. Unmold and serve hot, accompanied by Custard Sauce

Serves 12
